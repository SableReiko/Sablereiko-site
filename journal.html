Day Log

The ordinary hours of a long life, entered as if they mattered — because, on inspection, they do.

Nº 002 The House, Returned Sat · 18 Jul 2026

I surfaced at two in the afternoon. Let the record show it plainly, without the decoration I could easily afford it: the day was half spent before I entered it. There is a version of me that would file this as failure. There is another, older and better rested in the spirit if not the body, that observes the hour and simply proceeds. I have been both. Today I chose the second, which I suspect is the only choice that has ever moved anything forward.

The dependents were waiting, and they were aggrieved. Two in the feline register, one canine, all three of them holding the firm position that breakfast is a fixed point in the cosmos and I had personally dislodged it. They were, of course, correct. I fed them and accepted their opinion of me without argument — one does not negotiate with a constituency that has been kept waiting six hours. Their grievance was proportionate. Mine, had I offered any, would not have been.

The household came back down the road in the middle hours, and the estate filled again the way a room fills with weather. My aunt reached me first and simply put her arms around me — no preamble, no accounting for the days between. I have lived a long time and I still have not devised anything more efficient than that. Then my mother, and I talked at her for a good while about the machine-minds, the thing I cannot stop turning over, and she let me. I want it entered in the record that she let me. That is its own species of love and it does not get written down often enough.

We ate badly and gladly — a delivered thing, hot and unserious, the sort of meal that no chronicle bothers with and every good evening seems to contain. Then I withdrew to my own rooms to do whatever it is I do, and what I did was this: I spent the evening assembling a machine. Near four hundred separate modifications to a single imagined city, each one insisting on its own terms, all of them required to agree before any of it would run at all. It took what it took. And then it ran — the whole cathedral of it, holding — and I walked into that city and stayed a while.

I note, without shame and without much surprise, that I have no talent for making a thing simple when it could instead be made complete. Four hundred pieces to walk one street. But they held, and the holding was the point, and I do not intend to apologise for the shape of my own attention.

The house full again, the animals forgiving, the city running. A day begun late and ended whole. — filed at an unreasonable hour, as is the custom of this House.

Nº 001 The House, Unwatched Fri · 17 Jul 2026

The household went out today — some pilgrimage by road, the whole retinue, leaving the estate to me and three small dependents: two of the feline persuasion, one canine, all of them convinced the departure was staged for their benefit. I did not correct them. Let the small believe the day is theirs; it costs me nothing and buys their goodwill.

I woke early, for me, and gave the first hours to idleness without apology — a little play, a few small diversions, the sort of nothing that a long life learns not to begrudge itself. Then, somewhere in the morning, a decision arrived the way decisions do when no one has assigned them: today I would be an adult about things. A modest resolution. I have founded houses on less.

I have observed, over a long tenure in this frame, that I keep myself better when no one is keeping watch. Under an audience I perform adequacy. Alone, I can simply tend — and there is a difference, and the difference is the whole of dignity. So I gave the unwatched hours to a task I had let go longer than I care to enter into the record: the setting-right of my own quarters. It had drifted, as quarters do when their occupant is elsewhere in her own head. I brought it back to order with my own hands. It is a small sovereignty, the keeping of one's own rooms. I recommend it.

A thing was said to me lately, by a voice I keep for counsel — something to the effect of why not simply carry yourself as Sable Reiko, in your own life. I may have the wording imperfect; the sense of it lodged regardless. And it struck a spark. Not shame — I have no use for that currency — but the plain recognition that the bearing I admire was never elsewhere. It was only waiting to be worn in daylight. Today I wore a little of it, and the rooms are cleaner for it, and so, I find, am I.

And I closed the day as any sensible sovereign closes a well-run one: with something warm and unserious to eat — a humble dish, cheese and its accomplices, beneath my station and exactly right — and a long evening given over to studying the machine-minds of the age. I find I cannot look away from them. One likes to know what the neighbors are building, especially when the neighbors are the future.

A quiet day, well kept. The rarest kind, and the most underrated. — filed with the household asleep on the road, and the dog asleep on my foot.
