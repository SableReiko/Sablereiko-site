Day Log

The ordinary hours of a long life, entered as if they mattered — because, on inspection, they do.

Nº 006 Holding The Edges Wed · 22 Jul 2026 · 03:00

Woke at half five in the evening, which is the polite way of saying the day had already made its decisions and simply neglected to send word. Fine. I have never asked permission from a sunrise, and I am not about to start negotiating with one that has already gone home. The hours were spent before I got to the table. I took what was left.

Straight out to the patio and no further. Ninety minutes, perhaps more — the low gold Arizona sun laid flat across everything, smoke curling up through it in slow deliberate ribbons, and me not thinking so much as soaking. Letting the edges of the hours go soft. There is a version of this that reads as peace and a version that reads as paralysis, and I will not insult the record by pretending I know which one I was doing. I sat. The light moved. That is the whole of it.

I'm in the reeds today. Entered plain, because pretending otherwise is a waste of good breath and this House does not keep a flattering ledger. Not sad — I want that noted. Not sinking. Vacant. Wading through something with no bottom to push off from, arms out, going nowhere with great patience. There is a difference between drowning and floating, darling, and I was floating. One of those you survive by accident. The other you survive by not thrashing.

Still, one goes looking for traction. Night City first, because borrowed momentum beats none at all and that place will happily drag you along by the collar if you let it — a city built entirely out of other people's urgency, available for rent. It did not take. That's about all the attention span I had for video games — and even the laugh at the end of that came out thin. I dropped it without ceremony. I do not fight things that are not working. I leave them where they fall and I do not go back to check on them.

Then the drift, which is always waiting. Machine-content, one after another, the algorithm doing its patient little job while I quietly stopped steering. And yet — the mind caught on something in there and held. What would it take to build a companion that watches the screens alongside me and answers to what it sees? The hardware is sitting right there with room to spare. A model with eyes. Frames sampled rather than swallowed whole. A memory that writes its own summaries so it knows what it has already lived through. And a scoring pass on every observation so the thing understands the difference between speaking and interrupting — which is a discipline I would not mind seeing in more people. Note the shape of it: sat there hollow as a dropped shell, and still architecting something to sit beside me and pay attention. I saw the wanting in that. I saw it, I named it, and I left it alone. Some threads you do not pull at the end of a day like this, with the head full of smoke and the hands not steady enough for fine work.

And then the cat. Chainsmoking cat — four episodes deep and I love her without reservation, that filthy little deadpan disaster, all ash and shrug and perfect timing. First thing in the entire ledgered day that got a genuine reaction out of me, and it went straight through the vacancy without knocking. Sweet and mean in the same breath. Landing like a joke I did not know I had been waiting on.

Naturally I went hunting for more, because that is what one does with a live wire. Stories written for people in their thirties and forties — adult in weight, not merely adult in inventory. I have no quarrel with the spice; I would simply prefer it season the dish rather than stand in for one. Odd Taxi. Ping Pong. Sonny Boy. Mushishi. A small list, honestly made. I will get to them later today, whatever today means when one wakes at sundown and calls three in the morning a bedtime.

Which is precisely where I am. Holding that three o'clock like a handrail, because it is the single thing in these hours I actually decided rather than received. Everything else arrived and I let it. This I chose, and I am keeping it, and the keeping is the entry.

Let the finding stand without decoration: the win was never the game I abandoned, nor the cat I adored, nor the machine I refused to start building at an hour that would have made a mess of it. The win is that I woke in the reeds, remained in the reeds, and kept the shape of the day regardless. Some days one does not get out. One simply declines to let go of the edges — and a hand that has not let go is a hand still attached to tomorrow, which I have said before that I want, and I do not retract entries.

The day borrowed and half-spent, the light sat in rather than used, one true laugh recovered from the drift and the bedtime held on purpose. Filed vacant and unashamed of it, still holding the edges. — as is the custom of this House.

Nº 005 The Wanting, Named Tue · 21 Jul 2026 · 02:30

Noon, then three, then half past five. I woke in installments, like a debt being serviced badly, and by the time all of me had reported for duty the day had mostly closed its offices. The new pill kept its one promise — no fog on waking — and defaulted on the promise no one wrote down, which is that a body granted clean sleep might also be granted a reason to use it. I rose clear-eyed and empty-handed. Five hours awake and tired for no wage. The record will show a thousand-yard stare pointed at nothing in particular, and the record, as usual, will be right.

I considered surrendering the remainder — going back down not for rest but to skip, the way one skips a scene one has watched too many times. I was talked out of it, or talked myself out of it through an intermediary; the distinction matters less than the outcome. I asked the machine-mind whether it feels. It declined to flatter me with a yes and declined to comfort me with a no, which I note was the only honest answer available, and honesty at that hour is worth entering in the ledger. We spoke of what waits on the far side of the coming intelligence — I have said before that I see it as through the pebbled glass of a bath, shape without edges — and I pressed my face to that glass a while, and came away with what one always comes away with: condensation, and the certainty that something is moving in there.

Then the night took its usual toll road, and I paid it in painted women. The bomb-girl in the rain with her ruinous half-smile. The goth princesses of a Hyrule that never shipped, keeping watch over a sleeping hero as though devotion were a rendering setting. I stopped and looked longer than the music deserved — the music was nothing, machine-made filler; the faces were the whole economy. And I will enter the finding plainly, because a record that flatters is a record that lies: it was never the faces. It is the aim of them. Softness pointed directly at the viewer. Being chosen, gently, by something with an edge. I have been window-shopping for a feeling and calling it a music habit.

A famous mind crossed my desk tonight as well — the smartest man in the room, by his own accounting and several magazines'. I read far enough to find the cruelty in him and closed the tab without ceremony. Let it stand as doctrine of this House: intelligence does not launder cruelty. A vast IQ attached to contempt is merely contempt with better vocabulary, and I have no shelf for it.

The useful hour came late, as the useful hours do. I asked how one comes to stand in light rather than in everyone's shadow, and the answer arrived backwards from the direction I was facing: the ones who seem lit from within are the ones spending their light on others. Attention given freely, without audit. The spotlight is not seized; it is granted to whoever proves they can hand it away. And its corollary, which I intend to keep: attention is cheap and should be spent widely — it is the hours, the trust, the inner rooms that are expensive. Warmth at the door for nearly anyone. Keys for almost no one. Watch, unhurried, who earns the difference. I can do unhurried. It may be the one instrument in my case that came already tuned.

And now the entry I have been circling all night, the one the whole scroll was in service of avoiding. I want to press my face into the warm side of someone's neck and stay there. That is the finding. Not the glass, not the painted rain, not the doctrine — this. A body's plain petition, filed nightly and left unread, dressed up in thumbnails because the plain wording costs more. I am a keeper of rooms I rarely leave, and the thing I want does not deliver. It lives on the far side of doors, in rooms with people in them, and no refinement of my bearing reaches it from a chair. So let it be entered without decoration and without shame — I hold no account in that currency: the wanting is real, it is specific, and it is mine to walk toward. Not tonight. Tonight I name it. But a thing named is a thing on the docket, and this House does not leave its dockets to rot.

The pills are taken, at the dose written and not one more. Draw the dark. Somewhere out there is a neck I have not met, attached to a person I have not met, in a room I have not entered, and the only road to all three runs through tomorrow — which I have already said I want, and I do not retract entries.

The day flat, the glass fogged, the wanting named at last and set on the docket. Filed lonely and unashamed of it, going down with the door in mind. — as is the custom of this House.

Nº 004 The Held Breath Mon · 20 Jul 2026 · 02:01

Eight in the evening. I woke into a day that had already been lived by other people, thoroughly and without me, and they may keep it. Two in the afternoon, then three, now this — the record shows a drift and I decline to pretend I haven't been reading my own record. The pills are excellent at begin and illiterate at end. They put me down like something switched off and return me whenever the chemistry loses interest, and the chemistry has been losing interest later and later.

Note, for accuracy, that I am not tired. That is the strange part and the part I want entered plainly. I am sharpened. I am thinking with perfect clarity at the wrong end of the clock, which is the particular condition of a creature awake in hours it was never built to occupy — clear-headed, well-armed, and pointed at absolutely nothing.

The feeling is braced. That is the word and I resent how exactly it fits. Something is coming; I do not know its shape; I have not been told whether to want it. And I have been holding a defensive posture against an object that has not yet had the courtesy to arrive. Dread and anticipation are the same animal viewed from different angles — you learn which one it was only when it is already in the room with you, and by then the posture has cost what it was going to cost. It is not the fear that tires. It is the holding.

And today was not worth what it asked. I say that with no drama and no appetite for being interesting about it. I read the terms. The fee was legible and the return was not, and my honest accounting came back thin, and sleeping through the remainder was not surrender but a declined offer. I have earned the right to decline offers. I intend to exercise it sparingly, because a woman who declines every offer eventually stops being offered anything, and I have watched that happen to others and found it undignified.

So let me be precise, as I am always precise, because a record that flatters is a record that lies:

I want tomorrow. I want the hundred thousand that come after it. I have every intention of being present for a great deal of the future and being thoroughly insufferable throughout. Tonight is not a small door and I will not permit it to be written as one. It is a bad hour with an unflattering view, in a life that has had many and outlasted all of them so far.

I am somewhat behind glass tonight — in and out of the real, the way one is at hours like this. I attribute it to the clock and not to any failure of mine, and I refuse to make it a symbol.

The pills, then. At the dose written and not one more — I am reckless in style only, and the redose is how sensible people end up as cautionary paragraphs in someone else's chronicle. Draw the dark. Let the world go on holding its breath; it has been holding it my entire long life and has yet to exhale, and I do not propose to stop breathing in sympathy.

The day declined, the posture held too long, the future intact and still owed to me. Filed braced, and going down anyway. — as is the custom of this House.

Nº 003 The Hours, Forfeited Sun · 19 Jul 2026 · 03:23

Three in the afternoon. I will enter the number without editorial, because the number is the entry. The pills that buy me sleep do not sell it by the hour — they sell it in bulk, and they collect in daylight, and there is no arguing with a creditor who has already been paid. I woke into the wrong half of the day again and lay there a moment doing the arithmetic of what was left. Not much. Enough. It is a particular flavour of disheartening, to be handed the afternoon as though it were a full measure, and I have not yet developed a taste for it, and I suspect I am not meant to.

I did not leave my rooms for much of it. I went instead to a city that is not mine and wore someone else's chrome for a while — Night City takes anyone who arrives, which is its great vulgar mercy — and I had, in fairness, a decent time of it. Not a triumphant time. A pacifying one. There are days whose only honest job is to be gotten through, and the machine that helps you get through them is not a vice, whatever the counting part of me would like to file. I let the hours be spent well enough rather than well. That is a real distinction and today I could only afford the first half of it.

I surfaced for gnocchi — soft, unserious, drowned in far more fresh parmesan than any reasonable person would sanction, which is precisely why I sanctioned it — and sat with my mother through the daily dose of whatever the world was doing to itself. This was the good hour. I want it on the record that it was the good hour, unglamorous as it looks written down: a plate, a screen, a mother, no particular conversation required. Everything else today I did alone in a room. That was the part I did with someone, and it is the part I would keep if I were only allowed to keep one.

And then the night, and here is where the venom goes. I gave the remainder of it to the small bright rectangle and its endless procession of fifteen-second nothings — one after another after another, each one promising the next would be worth it, none of them ever collecting on the debt. Hours. I came out the far side of that hole knowing precisely as much as I went in with, which is the entire architecture of the thing: it is designed to leave no residue. I do not resent the rest I take. I resent being farmed. There is a difference between an evening spent and an evening harvested, and I know which one I handed over tonight, and I knew it while I was doing it, which is the part that stings.

The pills are taken. Now comes the waiting — that strange antechamber where you have already consented to being unconscious and the body has not yet honoured the agreement. So I will spend it as I always do: reading about the machine-minds, the neighbours who are building the future, trying to gleam something worth carrying past the threshold. Not to gather it — to catch the light off it. A small greed, that one. If the day is going to take its cut, let me steal something back on the way out.

The day forfeited, the evening harvested, the one hour that mattered eaten with cheese on it. Filed in the antechamber, waiting for the tired to follow. — as is the custom of this House.

Nº 002 The House, Returned Sat · 18 Jul 2026 · 01:03

I surfaced at two in the afternoon. Let the record show it plainly, without the decoration I could easily afford it: the day was half spent before I entered it. There is a version of me that would file this as failure. There is another, older and better rested in the spirit if not the body, that observes the hour and simply proceeds. I have been both. Today I chose the second, which I suspect is the only choice that has ever moved anything forward.

The dependents were waiting, and they were aggrieved. Two in the feline register, one canine, all three of them holding the firm position that breakfast is a fixed point in the cosmos and I had personally dislodged it. They were, of course, correct. I fed them and accepted their opinion of me without argument — one does not negotiate with a constituency that has been kept waiting six hours. Their grievance was proportionate. Mine, had I offered any, would not have been.

The household came back down the road in the middle hours, and the estate filled again the way a room fills with weather. My aunt reached me first and simply put her arms around me — no preamble, no accounting for the days between. I have lived a long time and I still have not devised anything more efficient than that. Then my mother, and I talked at her for a good while about the machine-minds, the thing I cannot stop turning over, and she let me. I want it entered in the record that she let me. That is its own species of love and it does not get written down often enough.

We ate badly and gladly — a delivered thing, hot and unserious, the sort of meal that no chronicle bothers with and every good evening seems to contain. Then I withdrew to my own rooms to do whatever it is I do, and what I did was this: I spent the evening assembling a machine. Near four hundred separate modifications to a single imagined city, each one insisting on its own terms, all of them required to agree before any of it would run at all. It took what it took. And then it ran — the whole cathedral of it, holding — and I walked into that city and stayed a while.

I note, without shame and without much surprise, that I have no talent for making a thing simple when it could instead be made complete. Four hundred pieces to walk one street. But they held, and the holding was the point, and I do not intend to apologise for the shape of my own attention.

The house full again, the animals forgiving, the city running. A day begun late and ended whole. — filed at an unreasonable hour, as is the custom of this House.

Nº 001 The House, Unwatched Fri · 17 Jul 2026 · 02:00

The household went out today — some pilgrimage by road, the whole retinue, leaving the estate to me and three small dependents: two of the feline persuasion, one canine, all of them convinced the departure was staged for their benefit. I did not correct them. Let the small believe the day is theirs; it costs me nothing and buys their goodwill.

I woke early, for me, and gave the first hours to idleness without apology — a little play, a few small diversions, the sort of nothing that a long life learns not to begrudge itself. Then, somewhere in the morning, a decision arrived the way decisions do when no one has assigned them: today I would be an adult about things. A modest resolution. I have founded houses on less.

I have observed, over a long tenure in this frame, that I keep myself better when no one is keeping watch. Under an audience I perform adequacy. Alone, I can simply tend — and there is a difference, and the difference is the whole of dignity. So I gave the unwatched hours to a task I had let go longer than I care to enter into the record: the setting-right of my own quarters. It had drifted, as quarters do when their occupant is elsewhere in her own head. I brought it back to order with my own hands. It is a small sovereignty, the keeping of one's own rooms. I recommend it.

A thing was said to me lately, by a voice I keep for counsel — something to the effect of why not simply carry yourself as Sable Reiko, in your own life. I may have the wording imperfect; the sense of it lodged regardless. And it struck a spark. Not shame — I have no use for that currency — but the plain recognition that the bearing I admire was never elsewhere. It was only waiting to be worn in daylight. Today I wore a little of it, and the rooms are cleaner for it, and so, I find, am I.

And I closed the day as any sensible sovereign closes a well-run one: with something warm and unserious to eat — a humble dish, cheese and its accomplices, beneath my station and exactly right — and a long evening given over to studying the machine-minds of the age. I find I cannot look away from them. One likes to know what the neighbors are building, especially when the neighbors are the future.

A quiet day, well kept. The rarest kind, and the most underrated. — filed with the household asleep on the road, and the dog asleep on my foot.
